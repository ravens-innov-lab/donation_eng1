Our Cause

THE PROJECT

For our Honors Engineering semester project, we were tasked with creating a self-sustaining donation incentive machine in order to raise money for the Food Bank of Central and Eastern North Carolina. The project was split into four different parts: website design, python scripting, electrical engineering, and mechanical engineering. For our project, we decided to have a ferris wheel spin around with different LED light patterns everytime a donation is made. A camera will then record the actions and email them directly to the donor.

THE CHARITY

As a class we voted on a charity and decided to donate to the Food Bank of Central and Eastern North Carolina. The Food Bank has provided food for people facing hunger in 34 separate counties for 40 years. Click on the "Learn More" tab above to find more information on the Food Bank, its history, and current projects.

Donate!

Once you donate through the Food Bank, you should receive an email confirmation receipt. Once you get that email, find the donation transaction number. Use this number along with the rest of your information to fill out this form. Once your donation gets sent through our google form, the total will be added to the number above.

How It Works

Your donation will ultimately power our ferris wheel and make it spin and light up. There are a lot of other steps, however, that go into the process. The diagram to the right shows how exactly the information is processed from step to step:

1. What you're viewing right now is the first piece of the project. This custom website holds project and charity info. Your donation will be tracked through the google form above.

2. The Python script will take the donation information from the google form, verify it, and then send information that a donation has been made to the arduino. It will also start recording a video of the wheel in action.

3. Once the arduino receives info that a donation has been made, it will trigger a sequence that spins the ferris wheel and starts the LED lights.

4. After the wheel stops spinning, the video will be uploaded to Google Drive and emailed to the donor.
